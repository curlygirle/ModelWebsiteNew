How does UI relate to interactivity: When we look at what UI and interactivity are they are defined as follows: UI is the graphical representation of a program or system that allows users to interact with it and Interactivity is how the user can interact with the UI and the system that it represents and to what degree. Therefore, a well-designed UI should be easy and fast, allowing users a full understanding of the features provided along with how to interact with them. The level of interactivity in the UI could have a major influence on the user experience, influencing how engaging and satisfying the interaction is for the user. An example of this is clear and easy-to-use navigation menus, clickable buttons, sliders, dropdown menus etc. which can help users to engage more actively with the website and achieve their outcomes more efficiently.

Reflect on Readings: The reading I have selected and gone through is titled ‘The Road to Technocolonialism’ by Giorgos Verdi. Technocolonialism refers to the influence and dominance that is exerted by technological powers over less developed regions. With this definition in mind we can see this kind of power being exerted by China and the United States to other countries across the world as their ‘success is largely due to their “domestic champions”, the multinational corporations that have come to be known as GAFAMI (Google, Apple, Facebook, Amazon, Microsoft, IBM) and BATX (Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent, Xiaomi) $3.3 trillion of market capitalization.’ The concept of techno-colonialism is introduced in the article, in which centralized control and ownership of technology are utilized to impose dominance. AI superpowers are expected to exercise influence on client governments, extending their political and economic might while getting access to vital data. This therefore highlights that the rise of AI will profoundly impact geopolitics in the 21st century. If uncontrolled, poor countries may be forced to choose between adopting techno-colonialism and entering a new dark era.
